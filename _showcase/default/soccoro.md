    <h2>Interreg 2 Seas – SOCORRO</h2>

    <h5>
        Seeking Out Corrosion, before it is too late
    </h5>

    <hr />

    <p>
        The <strong>SOCORRO</strong> project was a European Interreg 2 Seas initiative
        dedicated to the development of innovative monitoring strategies for
        corrosion-risk assessment in maritime and industrial infrastructures.
    </p>

    <p>
        The project aimed to provide reliable tools for detecting, monitoring,
        and anticipating corrosion processes in submerged and steel-based
        structures exposed to aggressive environmental conditions.
    </p>

    <h5>Funding</h5>

    <p>
        <strong>Programme:</strong> Interreg 2 Seas 2014–2020<br>
        <strong>Funding instrument:</strong> European Regional Development Fund (ERDF)<br>
        <strong>Total project budget:</strong> approximately <strong>5.45 M€</strong><br>
        <strong>ERDF contribution:</strong> approximately <strong>3.3 M€</strong><br>
        <strong>Project duration:</strong> March 2020 – September 2022

    <hr />

    <h5>Consortium</h5>

    <p>
        SOCORRO involved a consortium of European academic, industrial,
        and technological partners from Belgium, France, the Netherlands,
        and the United Kingdom.
    </p>

    <p>
        The project was coordinated by the
        <strong>Antwerp Maritime Academy</strong>.
    </p>

    <p>
        The consortium combined expertise in:
    </p>

    <ul>
        <li>Corrosion science</li>
        <li>Maritime engineering</li>
        <li>Electrochemistry</li>
        <li>Ultrasonic monitoring</li>
        <li>Structural health monitoring</li>
        <li>Environmental sensing and data analysis</li>
    </ul>

    <h5>Scientific Contribution</h5>

    <p>
        Within SOCORRO, the UPHF/IEMN contribution focused on ultrasonic
        approaches for corrosion monitoring using reverberated ultrasonic
        wavefields and <strong>Coda Wave Interferometry</strong>.
    </p>

    <p>
        These methods were investigated for their sensitivity to small
        mechanical changes induced by generalized corrosion processes
        in metallic structures.
    </p>

    <h5>Main Research Topics</h5>

    <ul>
        <li>Corrosion monitoring in maritime environments</li>
        <li>Coda Wave Interferometry</li>
        <li>Ultrasonic guided waves</li>
        <li>Structural health monitoring</li>
        <li>Diffuse and reverberated wavefields</li>
        <li>Electrochemical monitoring methods</li>
        <li>Temperature compensation strategies</li>
    </ul>

    <hr />

    <h5>Selected Results</h5>

    <ul>
        <li>
            Development of monitoring tools for submerged steel structures.
        </li>

        <li>
            Integration of electrochemical and ultrasonic monitoring approaches.
        </li>

        <li>
            Experimental validation of coda-wave methods for corrosion detection.
        </li>

        <li>
            Demonstration of monitoring strategies on industrial case studies.
        </li>
    </ul>
