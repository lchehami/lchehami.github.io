    <h2>ANR PANSCAN</h2>

    <h5>
        PANSCAN – Advanced Ultrasonic Inspection and Imaging Strategies
    </h5>

    <hr />

    <p>
        The <strong>PANSCAN</strong> project is an ANR-funded collaborative
        research initiative dedicated to advanced ultrasonic inspection,
        guided-wave imaging, and wave-based sensing strategies for
        nondestructive evaluation of complex structures.
    </p>

    <p>
        The project investigates innovative methodologies combining
        guided ultrasonic waves, beamforming, inverse problems,
        signal processing, and advanced instrumentation for
        structural health monitoring and defect characterization.
    </p>

    <h5>Funding and Programme</h5>

    <p>
        <strong>Programme:</strong> Agence Nationale de la Recherche (ANR)<br>
        <strong>Project reference:</strong> ANR-17-CE08-0013-01<br>
        <strong>Project acronym:</strong> PANSCAN<br>
        <strong>Funding framework:</strong> ANR Collaborative Research Project
    </p>

    <p>
        The project was funded through the French National Research Agency
        (ANR) under the CE08 programme dedicated to advanced engineering,
        wave physics, instrumentation, and sensing technologies.
    </p>

    <hr />

    <h5>Consortium</h5>

    <p>
        PANSCAN brought together academic research groups and engineering
        partners working on:
    </p>

    <ul>
        <li>Ultrasonic imaging</li>
        <li>Guided ultrasonic waves</li>
        <li>Beamforming and inverse methods</li>
        <li>Nondestructive testing (NDT/NDE)</li>
        <li>Structural health monitoring</li>
        <li>Wave propagation in complex media</li>
    </ul>

    <p>
        The consortium combined expertise in experimental ultrasonics,
        wave physics, signal processing, and imaging methodologies
        for industrial inspection applications.
    </p>

    <hr />

    <h5>Scientific Objectives</h5>

    <p>
        PANSCAN explored advanced wave-based sensing strategies
        for improving defect detection and imaging performance
        in complex and challenging inspection configurations.
    </p>

    <p>
        Particular attention was given to guided-wave propagation,
        robustness against complex geometries, and high-resolution
        reconstruction algorithms for industrial structures.
    </p>

    <h5>Main Research Topics</h5>

    <ul>
        <li>Guided ultrasonic waves</li>
        <li>Beamforming and inverse imaging</li>
        <li>Wave propagation in complex structures</li>
        <li>Passive and active ultrasonic sensing</li>
        <li>Signal processing for NDT/NDE</li>
        <li>Structural health monitoring</li>
    </ul>

    <hr />

    <h5>Expected Outcomes</h5>

    <ul>

        <li>
            Development of advanced ultrasonic imaging methodologies.
        </li>

        <li>
            Improved inspection capabilities for industrial structures.
        </li>

        <li>
            Robust reconstruction algorithms for guided-wave imaging.
